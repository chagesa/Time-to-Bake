New York Times Chocolate Chip Cookies

  • 2 cups minus 2 TB cake flour
  • 1 2/3 cups breads flour
  • 1 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 1/2 sticks (1 1/4 cups) margarine
  • 1 1/4 cup light brown sugar
  • 1 cup + 2 TB sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla
  • 1 1/4 lb chocolate disks (chips, chunks, bar)

Preheat oven to 350 degrees.

Sift flour, baking soda, baking powder and salt. Set aside.

Cream margarine and sugars together until very light, about 5 minutes. Add eggs one at a time. Stir in vanilla. Reduce speed to low, add dry ingredients and mix until just combined, 5-10 seconds. Drop chocolate pieces in and incorporated. (Press plastic wrap against dough and refrigerate for 24-36 hours.) Scoop out TB of cookie dough, flatten (and sprinkle with sea salt). Bake cookies for 12 minutes.

Honey Cookies

  • 2 3/4 cup flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 egg/li>
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3/4 cup canola oil
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • turbinado sugar for rolling

Preheat oven to 350 degrees.

Sift flour, baking powder, cinnamon, ginger & salt. Set aside.

In a bowl beat the egg and sugar until light and creamy. Add oil and beat until fully incorporated. Add honey and vanilla and beat until mixture is smooth and creamy. Stir in flour mixture.

Scoop out a tablespoon of dough, roll in into a ball and flatten. Coat in turbinado sugar. Place on cookies sheet.

Bake for 10-11 minutes. Allow to cool on baking sheet before moving to wire rack.
